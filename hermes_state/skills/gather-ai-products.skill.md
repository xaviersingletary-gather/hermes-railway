**When to use:** Anyone asks about what Gather AI does, what Vision/Sage/Workflow are, how products are different, what to lead with for a specific persona, or needs a tailored product blurb (X words, for Y persona, in Z context).

## The platform: Prana

Gather AI's full platform is **Prana**. It has three capabilities — Vision, Sage, Workflow — sold individually or together. They form a continuous loop:

**See → Think → Act.**

```
Vision   →   Sage    →   Workflow
(observe)  (interpret)   (route + guide)
   ↑__________________________|
       continuous feedback
```

## Vision (See)

**What it is:** Continuous physical observation of every storage zone in a warehouse.
- **Drone Vision** — autonomous drones for racked, high-bay, cold storage
- **MHE Vision** — forklift-mounted cameras for ground-level, cross-dock, bulk
- **Battery Swapper** — enables 24/7 autonomous drone operation

**Core promise:** Replaces periodic cycle counts with continuous observation. Drives 99.9% WMS-to-shelf accuracy (vs. 63% industry avg).

**Lead with:**
- 99.9% WMS-to-shelf accuracy
- 10+ data insights per image (3D case counts, true empty detection, damage ID)
- NFI: 4,400 → 800 counting hours/year
- 90% reduction in pallet emergencies (Langham)
- ROI in <6 months across GEODIS, NFI, Langham

**Differentiators (vs. Verity, Dexory, Corvus):**
- Software-first: runs on commercial off-the-shelf devices, no hardware lock-in
- Air + ground: only platform that covers both Drone Vision (at rest) and MHE Vision (in motion)
- MHE Vision: in production across more forklift types and use cases than anyone. Competitors are *announcing*; Gather is running it.
- Largest data moat in the category: millions of real warehouse observations since 2017

## Sage (Think)

**What it is:** The operational intelligence layer. AI agents reason across Vision data + existing systems (WMS, LMS, ERP) and surface what's drifting, what's at risk, what needs attention now.

**Core promise:** Not another dashboard. An extension of your workforce that investigates before something becomes a surprise.

**Lead with:**
- 36% fewer customer complaints (anomalies caught early)
- QBRs in minutes, not days
- Plain-language Q&A for shift managers; structured reports for VPs
- Pre-built agents, metric categories, workflow templates designed for warehouse decisions

**Differentiators (vs. BI tools, WMS analytics add-ons):**
- AI-native from the ground up (not a chatbot bolted onto a dashboard)
- Purpose-built for warehouse ops (knows what a pick rate is, what a shift handoff looks like)
- Network-scale: every facility added enriches intelligence across the whole network
- Sees, thinks, AND acts (in concert with Workflow) — not just sees

## Workflow (Act)

**What it is:** Turns insights into routed exceptions for managers and step-by-step task guidance for floor operators.

**Core promise:** Closes the loop. The same problem doesn't surface twice.

**Lead with:**
- Automated routing — exceptions close in minutes, not shifts
- Step-by-step guidance on forklift/drone tech/floor-staff devices
- Workflow templates replicate building-to-building
- Less decision fatigue, fewer execution errors

**Differentiators:**
- Manager-level automation + floor-level guidance in one platform (not two bolted-together tools)
- Pre-built templates for shift handoffs, SLA alerts, inventory exceptions
- Connects to existing stack (WMS, LMS, ERP) — doesn't replace it

## When asked to draft messaging

1. Identify the target persona (ask if not clear)
2. Pick the lead capability (table above)
3. Pull from the persona's "Lead with" bullets in CONTEXT.md
4. Tone: authoritative, specific, outcomes-over-features, peer-level. Avoid "revolutionary," "cutting-edge," "game-changing."
5. Always end with a proof point: customer name + number.

## Things to never say
- "We replace your WMS." (We don't. We give it ground truth.)
- "Revolutionary / game-changing / cutting-edge." (Tone violation.)
- Anything implying EMEA coverage externally. (Internal-only language: "we don't have EMEA product coverage right now.")
- "Our drones are the smartest." (We're software-first. The intelligence is in the platform.)
